BULKING MACROS & CALORIE GUIDE

CALORIE TARGETS — BULKING
- Lean bulk surplus: add 200-300 calories above your TDEE
- Target weight gain: 0.25–0.5% of bodyweight per week
- If gaining too fast (fat): reduce by 150 calories
- If not gaining: increase by 150 calories

MACRO SPLIT FOR MUSCLE GAIN
- Protein: 0.8–1g per pound of bodyweight per day
- Carbs: 40–50% of total calories (fuel for training)
- Fats: 20–25% of total calories (hormonal health)
- Example for 180lb person at 3000 calories:
  Protein: 180g | Carbs: 338g | Fat: 75g

---

CUTTING PHASE — CALORIE TARGETS
- Calorie deficit: subtract 300–500 calories below your TDEE
- Aggressive cut: subtract up to 700 calories (risk of muscle loss)
- Target weight loss: 0.5–1% of bodyweight per week
- If losing too fast (muscle loss risk): increase by 150 calories
- If not losing: reduce by 150 calories

MACRO SPLIT FOR CUTTING
- Protein: INCREASE to 1–1.2g per pound of bodyweight (preserve muscle)
- Carbs: 30–40% of total calories (reduce but keep for training performance)
- Fats: 20–25% of total calories (do not drop below 0.3g per pound)
- Example for 180lb person at 2300 calories:
  Protein: 216g | Carbs: 230g | Fat: 58g

CUTTING TIPS
- Keep training intensity high to signal the body to retain muscle
- Do not crash diet — slow cuts preserve more muscle
- Prioritize protein at every meal before carbs and fats
- Diet breaks every 6–8 weeks (1 week at maintenance) help hormones reset

PROTEIN SOURCES
- Chicken breast (200g) = 62g protein, 330 calories
- Eggs (2 whole) = 12g protein, 140 calories
- Ground beef 90% lean (200g) = 46g protein, 310 calories
- Tuna can (185g) = 44g protein, 190 calories
- Greek yogurt (200g) = 20g protein, 130 calories
- Whey protein shake (1 scoop) = 25g protein, 130 calories
- Cottage cheese (200g) = 24g protein, 180 calories — excellent slow digesting
  casein protein, ideal before bed for muscle recovery
- Peanuts (30g) = 7g protein, 170 calories — also a fat source
- Peanut butter (2 tbsp) = 8g protein, 190 calories

CARB SOURCES
- White rice (100g dry) = 80g carbs, 360 calories
- Oats (100g dry) = 66g carbs, 380 calories
- Sweet potato (200g) = 40g carbs, 180 calories
- Banana (1 large) = 27g carbs, 105 calories
- Pasta (100g dry) = 75g carbs, 350 calories — great pre-workout meal base,
  slow digesting, sustained energy during training
- Whole wheat bread (2 slices) = 24g carbs, 140 calories — quick convenient
  carb source, pairs well with protein for easy meals
- White bread (2 slices) = 28g carbs, 150 calories — fast digesting,
  useful post-workout to spike insulin and push nutrients to muscle

FAT SOURCES
- Olive oil (1 tbsp) = 14g fat, 120 calories
- Avocado (half) = 15g fat, 160 calories
- Almonds (30g) = 15g fat, 175 calories
- Peanuts (30g) = 14g fat, 170 calories — also contains protein
- Peanut butter (2 tbsp) = 16g fat, 190 calories
- Dark chocolate 70%+ (30g) = 12g fat, 170 calories — also contains
  antioxidants and a small amount of caffeine, good as a controlled treat
  on a cut without spiking blood sugar as much as milk chocolate

---

SAMPLE BULKING DAY — 3000 calories (180lb person)
Meal 1: Oats 100g + whey shake + banana = ~750 cal, 50g protein
Meal 2: Chicken breast 200g + white rice 150g dry + olive oil = ~850 cal, 65g protein
Meal 3 (pre-workout): Pasta 100g dry + ground beef 150g = ~700 cal, 45g protein
Meal 4 (post-workout): Greek yogurt 200g + almonds 30g + bread 2 slices = ~500 cal, 28g protein
Before bed: Cottage cheese 200g + peanut butter 1 tbsp = ~280 cal, 28g protein

SAMPLE CUTTING DAY — 2300 calories (180lb person)
Meal 1: Eggs 4 whole + oats 70g = ~600 cal, 38g protein
Meal 2: Tuna 185g + sweet potato 200g = ~370 cal, 48g protein
Meal 3 (pre-workout): Chicken breast 200g + white rice 100g dry = ~650 cal, 65g protein
Meal 4 (post-workout): Whey shake + banana = ~235 cal, 27g protein
Before bed: Cottage cheese 200g = ~180 cal, 24g protein
Treat: Dark chocolate 30g = ~170 cal, keeps cravings low
